Valentine’s Day: A Celebration of Love, Connection, and Community

Valentine's Day, celebrated annually on February 14th, is a global occasion dedicated to expressing love, affection, and appreciation. While primarily known for romantic love, it has expanded in many cultures to celebrate friendships and family bonds as well.

St. Valentine: The day is named after a 3rd-century martyr (or possibly multiple). One popular legend claims a priest named Valentine secretly performed marriages for soldiers after Emperor Claudius II banned them, believing single men made better warriors.

Lupercalia: Some historians link the date to the Roman festival of Lupercalia (February 13–15), a fertility celebration that the Church later "Christianized" by establishing St. Valentine’s Feast Day on February 14th

Romantic Evolution: The association with romance didn't fully take hold until the 14th century, influenced by poets like Geoffrey Chaucer, who linked the day to the start of the bird mating season.
