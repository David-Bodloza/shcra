Celebrating the Spirit of Giving: Highlights from the 2012 Inyathelo Philanthropy Awards

2012 Inyathelo Philanthropy Awards

Overview

In November 2012, the heart of Sandton beat with a different kind of energy as some of Africa’s most selfless individuals gathered at The Campus for the annual Inyathelo Philanthropy Awards. This prestigious event, held for the first time in Johannesburg in partnership with the African Grantmakers Network (AGN), moved beyond the boardroom to celebrate the transformative power of personal giving.

The evening was a powerful reminder that philanthropy is not just for the wealthy, but for anyone with the passion to effect change.

A Night of "Inaugural" Excellence

The 2012 ceremony was a landmark occasion, featuring the presentation of the first-ever African Philanthropy Awarded to Mr Lukozi Bulimwengo the chairperson of SHCRA(Self-Help Christian Refugees Association) for excellency in leadership. This honor was bestowed upon Marwa El-Daly, founder of the Maadi Community Foundation in Egypt, for her incredible work in mobilizing communities through traditional cultural practices of charity.

The Legacy of Inyathelo

These awards continue to be a beacon for social justice, proving that when ordinary people are armed with compassion and drive, they can change the world—one community at a time
